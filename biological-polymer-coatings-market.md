<p><p>The biological polymer coatings market comprises three primary types: polysaccharides, proteins, and lipid compounds. Polysaccharides, like chitosan and alginate, offer biocompatibility and biodegradability, making them suitable for drug delivery and food packaging. Proteins, such as collagen and gelatin, provide excellent adhesion and film-forming abilities, promoting tissue engineering and bioprocessing applications. Lipid compounds, including phospholipids and fatty acids, enhance barrier properties and reduce contamination, commonly used in pharmaceuticals and cosmetics. Together, these polymers support a range of innovative applications across various industries.</p></p>
